Project Overview

Workgen Solutions supported PepsiCo's Food (PFNA) and Beverage (PBNA) divisions by conducting structured image-based quality audits on merchandising work completed by Shiftsmart partners deployed across retail stores.

The program involved reviewing pre-task and post-task store images submitted by field partners to evaluate in-store execution quality — covering backstock access, aisle stocking, cooler replenishment, and product putaway.

Each image submission was evaluated against structured Yes/No annotation criteria, enabling precise identification of compliance gaps, discrepancies, and potentially fraudulent submissions.

Business Units Covered

PFNA — PepsiCo Foods North America
Frito-Lay snacks, chips, and food product merchandising
PBNA — PepsiCo Beverages North America
Pepsi, Mountain Dew, Gatorade, Starry & Propel beverage lines

Scope of Work

Photo Audit & Labeling

Reviewed pre-task and post-task store images submitted by Shiftsmart partners via the partner portal, applying structured Yes/No annotation criteria across each submission.

Multi-Section Coverage

Evaluated four task sections per shift: Backstock Access & Usage, Fully Stocked Aisles, Fully Stocked Coolers, and Backstock Putaway — each with distinct annotation criteria.

Brand Priority Classification

Categorized brands by priority levels — distinguishing PepsiCo priority brands (Pepsi, Gatorade, Mountain Dew, Starry, Propel) from competitor brands — to focus quality checks appropriately.

Fraud & Discrepancy Detection

Identified discrepancies such as mismatched sections, incorrect backroom photos, angled or out-of-scope images, and potentially fraudulent submissions to protect data integrity.

Annotation Criteria by Section

Each task section had specific, structured validation criteria applied during image review.

S1
Section 1: Backstock Access & Usage
Navigate to the backroom and locate the Pepsi backstock area
Approve (Yes) Criteria
  • PepsiCo CSD & Gatorade products are clearly visible
  • Photos were taken in the backroom
  • Photos feature PepsiCo products (majority Pepsi brands)
Reject (No) Criteria
  • Pepsi CSD & Gatorade products aren’t clearly visible
  • Photo was not taken in the backroom
  • Photo features competitor brands (Coca-Cola, Sprite, Powerade, Fanta)
S2a
Section 2a: Fully Stocked Aisles
Stock aisle section per assigned index
Image Quality Criteria
  • Before & After photos are of the same aisle section
  • Photos directly face the shelf, clearly showing products
  • Each photo covers exactly one shelf ‘break’ section
Stocking Compliance Criteria
  • Products stocked tag-to-tag with no gaps
  • Products pulled to the front of the shelf (fronted)
  • Only PepsiCo brands stocked — no competitor placement
S2b
Section 2b: Fully Stocked Coolers
Stock assigned cooler section per index
Image Quality Criteria
  • Before & After photos show the same cooler unit
  • Photo directly faces the cooler, full unit visible top to bottom
  • Single cooler framed per photo — no multi-cooler shots
Stocking Compliance Criteria
  • Products stocked tag-to-tag and standing upright
  • Products pulled to the front of cooler shelves
  • Only PepsiCo brands stocked — no competitor products
S3
Section 3: Cart in Stocking Area
Photo of cart in the beverage aisle each time partner reloads from backroom
Approve (Yes) Criteria
  • At least 2–3 photos submitted of the cart in the stocking area
  • Products on cart are visibly different across photos (new load each time)
  • Photos taken in the beverage aisle or near the coolers (U-Boat or L-Cart used)
Reject (No) Criteria
  • Only 1 photo submitted of the cart
  • Products on cart look identical across all photos (no reload evidence)
  • Photos taken in the backroom, not in the stocking area
S4
Section 4: Backstock Putaway
Return cart and remaining Pepsi product to the backstock area
Approve (Yes) Criteria
  • PepsiCo CSD & Gatorade products are clearly visible
  • Photos taken in the backroom
  • Majority of products are PepsiCo brands
Reject (No) Criteria
  • Pepsi CSD & Gatorade products aren’t clearly visible
  • Photo not taken in the backroom
  • Majority of products are competitor brands
